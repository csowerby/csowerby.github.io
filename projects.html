Eigenmode Solver - Spring 2021

This paper presents my work in modifying a linear eigenmode solver to incorporate electro-magnetic effects in magnetically confined plasma. This was written as a part of UCLA Physics Departmental Honors Program.

Previously, this eigenmode solver used an electrostatic approximation in which the magnetic field was assumed to be fixed and uniform. This program uses a simplified version of the Braginskii two-fluid equations solved using finite differencing methods todetermine the frequency of wave instabilites as eigenvalues of the system. The eigenmode solver program uses numerical methods from the BOUT framework developed in the 1990â€™s for plasma modeling. The new eigenmode solver gives us a more accurate picture of instabilities in plasma, particularly with high β.

Relay Controlled Adjustable Resistor - Spring 2019

As part of my research for the Basic Plasma Science Facility at UCLA, I worked on developing a solution to the problem of eliminating noise in measuring the electron and ion saturation current in Langmuir probes.

When sweeping through voltages, it is difficult to measure both the ion and electron saturation currents with high accuracy. Since the ion and electron saturation currents are very different in magnitude, measuring the voltage drop across the same resistor is less precise due to a significant amount of noise from the ion current. If it would be possible to use different size resistors to measure the voltage drop, the data would be significantly easier to analyze.

My solution to this problem is a relay-controlled circuit to switch between several fixed valued resistors connected in parallel. Only one of the resistors was to be connected at once, so the saturation current would pass through the circuit over a resistor of chosen value by the user or the DAQ system. The power for the circuit as well as the signals to control the relays were sent from a Raspberry Pi via a DB9 connection to the outside of the circuit box.
